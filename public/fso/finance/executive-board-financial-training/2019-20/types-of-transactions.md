# Types of transactions

There are two basic types of Fulton account transactions that FSOs can make:

| Funds Request | Reimbursement |
| --- | --- |
| Payment or invoice to be paid | President, Treasurer OR approved organization member makes purchases with personal funds |
| Request to use the p-card |   |
| Approval to use funds for travel (processed as a reimbursement) |   |

## Payment or purchase order to pay a bill

- Potential PO&#39;s: ASU Catering, University Club, Memorial Union, Student Union, Anton, Artcraft Inc. Your vendor isn&#39;t one of these? Contact [fso@asu.edu](mailto:fso@asu.edu)to confirm if you can use them.
  - Submit the invoice to be paid, FRF, BMF (if paying for food and/or beverages) and anticipated attendee roster to FSE Student Engagement at least a week before the event
  - FSE Student Engagement checks your account to ensure you have the funding for the expense. If you do, it will be sent onward for processing. If you don&#39;t, you will be notified to find an alternative funding source.
  - Within ne business day after the event, the FSO submits the final attendee roster to the business office.
  - The bill is processed

## P-card policies

- The p-card is a privilege, not a right. Leaders who abuse it will not be allowed to use it again.
- FSOs are strongly encouraged to request to use the p-card when possible. It is one of the easiest and most efficient ways to manage your money.
- Only trained FSO officers may request to check out the p-card for allowable purchases. They may NOT pass the p-card off to other members.
- The p-card is available for the following time frames:
  - One hour: online purchases or on-campus purchases
  - Two hours: off-campus purchase
  - It is not available for overnight or weekend use.
- When using the p-card, you must return the p-card to the Business Office.
- You must also return all original receipts within one business day to the Business Office.
- If you receive online confirmations and/or receipts, email them to [fsoadmin@asu.edu](mailto:fsoadmin@asu.edu) and to [Velez@asu.edu](mailto:Tonali.Velez@asu.edu).

## P-card use tax

- ASU is responsible for paying state taxes. When an out-of-state vendor does not charge tax, the p-card will be charged a 5.6% p-card use tax to replace the tax that was not charged.
- This occurs with goods, not services.

## How to request to use the p-card

**Note: Submitting p-card requests ahead of time is highly encouraged. Last minute requests may not be approved.**

1. Submit your request online.
2. FSE Student Engagement will contact you with questions and/or approval.
3. Meet with the Business Office. Sign and submit all paperwork during the appointment.
4. You must get all receipts to the Business Office within 2 business days. If you do not receive receipts within that timeframe, you are responsible for contacting the vendor in a timely manner for all receipts for your purchases.

## P-card restricted purchases

- The p-card CANNOT be used for several specific items. See what is not allowable: P-card restricted purchases.
- Restricted items FSO leaders need to especially be aware of include:
- Car Rentals, Postage, Explosives
- Gifts, Contributions, Donations,
- Gift cards of any kind
- Hotels and lodging

## Reimbursements

- Reimbursements for individuals take time to process. Request approval before you make a purchase – we cannot guarantee it will be approved afterward if you do not receive approval.
- Reasons a reimbursement request will not be approved:
  - It was not submitted within 30 days after the transaction was completed (including mileage)
  - It&#39;s not an allowable purchase
  - Items were not shipped to an ASU address
  - The request is greater than $1,000
  - It involves alcohol

### Reimbursement documentation: Materials and/or supplies

- Cmpleted Funds Request/Reimbursement Form (FRF)
- Original paid itemized receipt(s) for all requested reimbursement items.
  - Hard copy (like Lowe&#39;s): Tape receipt to a sheet of paper. Do not tape over amounts.
  - Online (like Ebay): provide the printed online receipt
- Bank/Credit card statement or screenshot of the transaction with requestor&#39;s name on it and charges marked with an &quot;x&quot;. If your name is not on the banking transaction, you may make a copy of your credit card and black out all but the last four digits of the card and your name.

### Reimbursement documentation: Food and/or beverages

- Completed Funds Request/Reimbursement Form (FRF)
- Original paid itemized receipt(s) for all requested reimbursement items.
  - Hard copy (like Domino&#39;s): Tape receipt to a sheet of paper. Do not tape over amounts.
  - Online (like dominos.com): provide the printed online receipt
- Bank/Credit card statement or screenshot of the transaction with requestor&#39;s name on it and charges marked with an &quot;x&quot;. If your name is not on the banking transaction, you may make a copy of your credit card and black out all but the last four digits of the card and your name.
- Complete roster of attendees (event name, date, location; attendees&#39; names and role with the university, i.e. faculty, students, staff, industry, etc.)

### Reimbursement documentation: Mileage (in-state, not overnight)

- Complete Funds Request/Reimbursement Form (FRF)
- Complete the In State Travel Form for mileage/parking fees for reimbursement
- Round trip map of the drive. For your starting and ending point, you&#39;ll put your primary campus
  - Tempe: ASU Brickyard: 699 S. Mill Ave, Tempe, AZ
  - Poly: ASU Santan Hall: 7231 E. Sonoran Arroyo Mall, Mesa, AZ
- Anyone with an ASU affiliate ID automatically has a Workday account (it&#39;s ASU accounting system). When your mileage reimbursement is going through processing you will be asked to approve your request. You&#39;ll receive an email from Workday. The email should include a link to your Workday inbox, so simply click on the link. If you do not have this email, you can access your Workday inbox using [this link](https://www.myworkday.com/asu/d/home.htmld). Once you approve in Workday, your reimbursement should only take a few days to go through.

### Reimbursement documentation: Mileage (out-of-state, not overnight)

- Complete Funds Request/Reimbursement Form (FRF). Explain in the public purpose why you are driving if it is more expensive than the cost of airfare. Possible reasons could be:
  - It&#39;s more economical to carpool than fly.
  - You&#39;re pulling a large competition piece to the competition.
- Complete the In State Travel Form for mileage/parking fees for reimbursement
- Round trip map of the drive. For your starting and ending point, you&#39;ll put your primary campus
  - Tempe: ASU Brickyard: 699 S. Mill Ave, Tempe, AZ
  - Poly: ASU Santan Hall: 7231 E. Sonoran Arroyo Mall, Mesa, AZ
