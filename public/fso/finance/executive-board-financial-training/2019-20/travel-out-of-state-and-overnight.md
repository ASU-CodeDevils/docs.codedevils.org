# Travel: out-of-state and/or overnight

## Travel policies

- Travel status – a condition in which an individual is conducting necessary university business outside a 50-mile radius from his/her residence and/or designated duty post with supervisory approval.
- If students don&#39;t follow polices and timelines, the FSO will not be able to use travel funding and future funding expenditures will be frozen (no reimbursements, p-card or future travel).
- Students who do not submit a trip request prior to traveling will not be approved
- Travel claims not finalized within 30 days after return will not be processed.
- All traveling students must submit deliverables prior to reimbursements being approved.

## Multiple travel funding sources?

- If your organization receives funding from multiple ASU departments, you are responsible for notifying these departments of who your other funding contacts are and how much you have been funded.
- Providing the correct contact information is essential to make your travel experience go as smoothly as possible.
- Typically, the unit that gives the most funds processes the claim.

## Using the p-card for travel expenses

- Anything purchased with the p-card is from your organization&#39;s total travel budget. It may only be approved for the amount that your org was approved for travel funding.
- Using the p-card does not mean you will be reimbursed for more money.

| **The p-card may be used for these travel-related expenses:** | **The p-card**  **cannot**  **be used for these travel-related expenses:** |
| --- | --- |
| Registration that doesn&#39;t include lodging | Registration that includes lodging |
| Refundable airfare (i.e. if you cancel your flight, ASU can get the funding back) | Non-refundable airfare |
|   | Hotel/lodging |
|   | Rental cars |

## Ground Transportation

### Using personal vehicles

- Students are discouraged from using personal vehicles for FSO-related travel.
- When a personal vehicle must be used for organization travel, the driver assumes all liability associated with the trip.
- Drivers and all passengers should follow the following guidelines when using personal vehicles…
  - Drivers and passengers are expected to follow University policy and federal, state, and local laws and posted signs.
  - Drivers must have a valid operator&#39;s license.
  - Drivers must possess adequate auto insurance as required by law.
  - Drivers must possess current vehicle registration.
  - Drivers and all passengers shall have access to and use seatbelts and/or other approved safety restraint devices.
  - Drivers and all passengers shall not possess, consume or transport alcohol and/or illegal substances.
  - Drivers should take care to allow for adequate rest periods/drive times.
  - Drivers should assess the safety of the vehicle they are driving.

### Renting a vehicle

- If you are requesting reimbursement for a rental car, you must submit a justification for why you needed a rental car.
- Car rentals: cannot rent 12-15 passenger vans
- Required for Car Rental Drivers: you must take the ASU Defensive Driving in-person course before travelling.
  - You can see the class options [here](https://cfo.asu.edu/ehs-defensive-driving-training). Bring the completion certificate and your driver&#39;s licenses to your pre-departure appointment! Only drivers can be reimbursed, not passengers.
- Car Rentals: **reimbursement only** , p-card cannot pay for it. You must decline the additional insurance. Be sure to check the approved vendors [list](https://cfo.asu.edu/purchasing-sunmart)for which one to use!

## Hotel/lodging

- Learn about ASU hotel policies here: [https://www.asu.edu/aad/manuals/fin/fin501.html](https://www.asu.edu/aad/manuals/fin/fin501.html)
- Prior to travelling, work out among your organization how you will pay for your hotel room(s). Each traveler should only pay for the room they are staying in for the trip. ASU cannot reimburse travelers who pay for multiple rooms during a trip.
- Use Per Diem Rates: U.S Federal Government sets these amounts for how much you can be reimbursed for travel expenses, especially for food and lodging
  - Use [these rates](https://cfo.asu.edu/travel-perdiem)for deciding where to stay. If you go over it, you&#39;ll write a justification (ex: stayed at a conference-recommended hotel).
  - If you&#39;re requesting reimbursement for food, Concur will set those rates for you.
