## Purpose of the FSO Executive Board Financial Training

- Understand ASU Policies
- Navigate financial paperwork and processes
- Learn how to manage FSO funding sources, including how to request a p-card, reimbursement, payment to be made, or travel
- Understand event-planning and fundraising policies

## Why become an FSO?

- A student organization may apply to become a Fulton Student Organization (FSO). See how: [fso.engineering.asu.edu/how-being-an-fso-works/](https://fso.engineering.asu.edu/how-being-an-fso-works/)
- FSOs who receive and/or have funding in the Ira A. Fulton Schools of Engineering (FSE) are expected to work with FSE Student Engagement to use their funding.
- Student Engagement offers the following services and support to FSOs:
  - Opportunity to apply for Dean&#39;s Funding and other funding, as applicable
  - Promotion through the [Fulton Student Organization Directory](https://fso.engineering.asu.edu/student-org-directory/)
  - Opportunities to develop your FSO in regards to superior leadership, engagement, outreach and professional preparedness (i.e. advising, training, professional development, networking, etc.)
  - Advocacy by FSEand Fulton Student Council
  - Opportunities to be recognized when the organization leads in awards, scholarships, rankings, competitions and sponsorships

## FSO budget policies and expectations

- The president and/or treasurer is responsible for approving all purchases or reimbursements.
- The FSO is responsible for tracking all expenses and allocating amounts to the correct funding source and account. (i.e. Dean&#39;s Funding, USG funding, donations, etc.)

## Terms to know

| **FSE**  **Student Engagement Office** | Your point of contact for all FSO concerns, funding approvals and processes. Your liaison between various offices and departments to support your success. This office is primarily in Tempe-ECF 130 and Poly-Picacho 244. |
| --- | --- |
| **FSE**  **Business Office** | Processes FSO financial paperwork after receiving the approval from Student Engagement. This office is primarily in Tempe-ECG 251. |
| **Fulton Student Organization (FSO)** | Organizations who have applied for and become approved as FSOs. They may receive FSE funding. |
| **Purchasing Card (_P-card_)** | Trained officers may request to check out a p-card for specific requests. Requests are managed by Student Engagement. |
| **Reimbursement** | A member may pay for something out-of-pocket and request to be reimbursed from an FSO&#39;s account. Approval depends upon the kind of request and the type of funding requested. |
| **Payment/invoice to be paid** | A vendor may provide a bill or an invoice to be paid that the FSO then requests approval from Student Engagement for processing. |
| **Donation** | Individuals or corporations may donate funding to support an FSO. The FSO is responsible for using the funding according to the donor&#39;s intent. |
| **Funds Request Reimbursement Form (FRF)** | This form is required for any payment request or reimbursement (such as materials and supplies or mileage). |
| **Business Meals Form (BMF)** | This form is required for any purchase that involves food and/or beverages. |
| **Public Purpose** | A justification for purchases, which is required for every transaction using ASU funding, including p-card requests, reimbursements, invoices to be paid, and travel. |
| **Administrative Service Charge (ASC)** | 8.5% fee that affects transactions in all accounts EXCEPT for donation accounts. Transactions affected include p-card, reimbursements, invoices to be paid and travel. |
| **P-Card Use Tax** | 5.6% fee that hits an account when an out-of-state vendor does not charge tax. This is for goods, not services. Most common with online vendors. |
| **Per Diem** | The U.S. Federal Government sets these amounts for how much one can be reimbursed for travel expenses, especially for meals and hotel/lodging. |
| **Mileage** | ASU does not reimburse for gas. Mileage is 44.5 cents/mile. |
| **Recognition item** | Recognizing someone for service/leadership with an item, such as a trophy. |
| **Workday** | ASU&#39;s accounting system. |
